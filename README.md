| **University of California, Berkeley (UC Berkeley)** | UC Berkeley demonstrates technological readiness for the DOJ April 2026 mandate. The university adopts WCAG 2.1 AA as its governing technical standard and applies it comprehensively across websites, online courses, podcasts, and video platforms. Its procedures outline detailed accessibility requirements, including accurate captions, transcripts, alt text, keyboard navigation, and proper HTML structure. Extensive developer guidance, mandatory training materials, and clear remediation timelines further strengthen technological capacity. | UC Berkeley’s organizational readiness is supported by formal, enforceable procedures and a clearly defined governance structure. The Digital Accessibility Program (DAP) oversees policy implementation, with a designated Web Accessibility Coordinator providing leadership. Responsibilities are clearly assigned to faculty, staff, and contractors. Mandatory training, structured exception management, and systematic remediation workflows demonstrate strong institutional coordination. | Environmental readiness is driven by direct legal obligations under a DOJ Consent Decree and long-standing ADA Title II and ITAP requirements. Public complaint portals, required 7-day response windows, and defined remediation timelines reinforce accountability. Berkeley also clarifies institutional responsibility versus third-party platform constraints. |
| **University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA)** | UCLA demonstrates technological readiness for the April 2026 WCAG 2.1 AA mandate. The university explicitly acknowledges DOJ requirements and provides instructors with concrete examples such as alt text, captions, transcripts, and accessible PDFs. Ally analytics are used to identify accessibility gaps, though emphasis is primarily on course materials rather than systemwide platforms. | UCLA displays organizational readiness supported by a multi-unit governance structure, including the Teaching and Learning Center, Bruin Learn Center of Excellence, and Disabilities and Computing Program. A new Digital Accessibility Governance structure is under development. Support includes asynchronous modules, live training, consultations, and coordinated communications. | Environmental readiness is driven by explicit citation of the DOJ April 24, 2026 WCAG 2.1 AA mandate. Alignment with ADA and UCOP policy reflects regulatory engagement. Campuswide communications indicate transparency, and Ally analytics signal responsiveness to compliance pressure. |
